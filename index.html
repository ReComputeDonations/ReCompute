Our Mission

As students, we've immersed ourselves in various classes and extracurricular activities such as Robotics and Computer Science. Over Time we have noticed that these activities require access to computers that are more powerful than the regular school-issued Chromebooks. To learn and practice coding effectively, a computer that meets certain standards is essential. Unfortunately, we discovered that many of our peers lacked the opportunity to use such computers at home.

The COVID-19 pandemic exacerbated this issue, revealing that many more students faced this problem than previously thought. In Massachusetts alone, as of 2020, 30,000 students did not have access to a computer screen necessary for excelling in their studies both inside and outside the classroom.

At ReCompute, we're driven by a vision of equity in access to technology. Everyone, regardless of background or economic status, should have the opportunity to harness the transformative power of computers. Our mission is simple yet profound: to help bridge the digital divide by refurbishing donated computers and distributing them to underserved communities, schools, and individuals.

How Are We Going to Do This?

Our process involves multiple detailed phases to ensure a comprehensive approach:

Phase 1: Initial Deployment at Marlborough High School

  • Assessment: Conduct a needs assessment to identify students who lack access to adequate computers.
  • Collection: Collect at least 30 computers from local businesses, community members, and organizations.
  • Refurbishment: Fully reset each computer, install a new copy of a free Linux distribution, and ensure each device undergoes a rigorous testing process.
  • Distribution: Distribute the refurbished computers to identified students, accompanied by a guide on how to use the new system.

Phase 2: Expansion to Neighboring Towns

  • Outreach: Contact neighboring towns and schools to gauge interest and identify additional students in need.
  • Partnerships: Establish partnerships with local businesses and organizations to increase computer donations and financial support.
  • Scaling Up: Increase the volume of collected and refurbished computers based on the expanded needs assessment.
  • Workshops: Conduct workshops for recipients to help them get the most out of their new computers, covering basics of the Linux operating system and introductory programming.

Phase 3: Building a Volunteer Network

  • Recruitment: Actively recruit volunteers, including students, community members, and tech enthusiasts, to help with collection, refurbishment, and distribution.
  • Training: Provide comprehensive training for volunteers to ensure they are equipped with the necessary skills and knowledge.
  • Community Engagement: Engage with the community through events, information sessions, and collaborative projects to raise awareness and garner support.
  • Feedback Loop: Establish a feedback mechanism to continuously improve our processes based on recipient and volunteer experiences and suggestions.

How Can Companies Help Us?

We invite companies to join us in our mission to bridge the digital divide. Your support can make a significant difference in the lives of students in need. Here are some ways you can help:

  • Donate Unused Computers: Provide us with unused or old computers that can be refurbished and given a new life.
  • Financial Assistance: Contribute funds to help us with the refurbishment process, including replacing defective parts and installing new software.
  • Partnerships: Partner with us to expand our reach and impact. This can include co-hosting events, facilitating workshops, or providing logistical support.
  • Employee Engagement: Encourage your employees to volunteer with us, offering their skills in technology, education, or community outreach.

Together, we can empower the next generation with the tools they need to succeed in a digital world. Thank you for considering a partnership with ReCompute.
